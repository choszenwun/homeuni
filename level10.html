The Dimension

What Is the Astral Plane?

Think of reality as layers. The physical plane (3D) — where your body exists, dense, governed by physical laws. The astral plane (4D–5D) — where consciousness can operate without body, less dense, fluid, governed by different laws (thought equals instant movement, time is non-linear, walls don't block you). Higher planes (6D+) — purely consciousness-based, where guides and advanced beings operate.

The astral plane is the bridge between physical and higher dimensions. It's where dreams happen (your consciousness dips into astral during sleep — usually unconsciously), where certain entities operate, and where you can travel consciously through out-of-body experience.

Key point: the astral plane is as real as physical reality. You've been there before — every time you dream. Astral projection means going there consciously, with full awareness and control.

The Pioneer

Robert Monroe

Not a guru. Not a mystic. A businessman — radio executive, entrepreneur, pragmatic and sceptical. In 1958, at age 42, he spontaneously began having out-of-body experiences. He thought he was going insane. Doctors found nothing wrong.

Instead of dismissing it, he studied it. Like a scientist. He documented over 3,000 OBEs, tested variables, developed replicable techniques, founded the Monroe Institute (still operating), and published three books: Journeys Out of the Body, Far Journeys, and Ultimate Journey.

He proved OBE is repeatable (he did it thousands of times), teachable (the Monroe Institute has trained thousands), and measurable (brain activity during OBE is distinct from sleep or dreaming). That's why the CIA and U.S. military funded his research. That's why you're learning his method.

The Gateway

The Pineal Gland

The pineal gland sits at the centre of the brain. Mainstream science: it produces melatonin and regulates sleep. Esoteric traditions: it's the seat of the soul, the gateway through which consciousness enters and exits the body, the "third eye."

Dr. Rick Strassman hypothesised that the pineal gland produces DMT (dimethyltryptamine) — a naturally occurring compound found in plants, animals, and humans. His theory (still unconfirmed but influential): DMT releases at birth (consciousness enters body), at death (consciousness exits body), and in smaller amounts during deep meditation, breathwork, and altered states. This would explain why near-death experiences are so vivid — the gateway partially opens.

Practical implication: the healthier your pineal, the easier altered states become. Filter your water (fluoride accumulates in the pineal), get morning sunlight, eat clean (Level 0 diet applies here), and meditate regularly.

Basics

What Is Astral Projection?

Your consciousness leaves your physical body and operates independently. You're tethered by what Monroe called the "silver cord" — an energetic connection. You can return instantly by thinking "body." Your body continues breathing and functioning normally.

It's not dangerous if you know what you're doing. You can't get "stuck." Fear is the only real risk — low frequency attracts low-frequency encounters. It's not the same as lucid dreaming (lucid dream = aware you're dreaming, still in mind; AP = consciousness operating in non-physical dimension). Astral projection typically feels more real than waking life — hyper-vivid, hyper-aware.

The Method

How to Astral Project

Monroe tested hundreds of techniques over decades. This is the method that works most consistently.

Step 1: Focus 10 (Mind Awake, Body Asleep)

The gateway state. Your body is in sleep paralysis (completely relaxed, unmoving), your mind is fully awake.

Setup: lie down comfortably, dark room or eye mask, no distractions, headphones on if using Hemi-Sync audio. Process: relax progressively (tense then release each muscle group from toes to face). Deepen with breath (4-7-8 breathing: inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8 — repeat 10 times). Notice the body becoming heavy, numb, distant. Stay mentally alert — if you drift, open eyes briefly, then refocus.

Signs you've reached Focus 10: body feels like lead, tingling sensations, slight internal buzzing, feeling of floating while body is still. This may take weeks of practice. Be patient.

Step 2: Vibrations (The Exit Signal)

Once stable in Focus 10, you'll feel vibrations — intense energy surging through your body. Feels like electrical current, an earthquake inside you, intense buzzing. This is normal. This is the signal you're about to separate. Do not panic. If you react with fear, you'll snap back immediately. Accept the vibrations, mentally intensify them, wait for the peak. The peak is your exit window.

Step 3: Separation

Roll-Out: imagine rolling sideways off the bed — pure mental intention, no physical muscles. Lift-Out: imagine floating up like a balloon. Sit-Up: imagine sitting up while your body stays lying down. When separation happens, you'll know — it's unmistakable. Suddenly you're free, floating, hyper-conscious. You may see your body below you. Stay calm. Observe.

Step 4: Stabilise and Explore

First OBE usually lasts 10–60 seconds (excitement or fear pulls you back). To extend: stay calm, focus on environment (this stabilises the projection), move slowly. You can fly (just think "up"), travel instantly (think of a location), pass through solid objects, visit other dimensions (think "Focus 27" or "meet my guide"). To return: think "body" — instant return.

The Deep Pattern

What You'll Encounter

When you leave your body, you will meet beings. Monroe documented these extensively.

Guides and Higher Self

Spirit guides: beings from higher dimensions assigned to help you. They feel warm, loving, safe. Often appear humanoid or as light-beings. Communicate telepathically. To meet yours, ask in astral: "I want to meet my guide." Trust first impression.

Higher Self (I-There): Monroe's term for your larger consciousness. You in body are one expression of something much bigger — all incarnations, all timelines, integrated. It shows you your life from higher perspective, gives mission clarity, downloads information directly. To meet it: ask in astral, "I want to meet my Higher Self." You might see a radiant future version of you, or simply merge with it temporarily — overwhelming knowing and love.

Deceased loved ones: souls in Focus 22–27. Usually healed, younger-looking than at death. Conversations are telepathic. This is experiential proof that consciousness continues after death.

Loosh and Parasitic Entities

Monroe's most disturbing discovery (documented in Far Journeys): some entities harvest human emotional energy — specifically fear, suffering, and trauma. He called this energy loosh. He observed beings farming humans the way humans farm livestock. This is uncomfortable to consider, but it's what Monroe reported after decades of rigorous investigation.

Low-level parasites feel heavy, dark, draining — shadow-like, amorphous, non-communicative. They feed on energy like leeches. Higher-level entities (Monroe encountered these too) are more intelligent, more manipulative. Level 11 will go deeper into who they are and how they operate.

Protection Through Frequency

Parasitic entities operate at low frequencies — fear, suffering, trauma. If you're vibrating at those frequencies, they can access you. If your frequency is high enough, they literally cannot touch you. Not because you fought them — because you transcended their range.

Everything from Levels 0–9 has been raising your frequency. Clean vessel, integrated shadow, full charge, aligned with purpose. By the time you reach this level, your baseline frequency is high. When you encounter something hostile in astral: don't fight (engaging lowers your frequency to their level), don't run (fear lowers frequency). Recognise it's operating at fear frequency. Raise your own — visualise light, feel love and sovereignty. Command it to leave. Or simply ascend dimensionally. It can't follow.

Monroe's Ultimate Discovery

In Ultimate Journey, Monroe's deepest conclusion: there is no death. Consciousness continues. You are a fragment of something much larger (the I-There). When you die, you don't end — you rejoin. Like a drop returning to ocean. You were never separate, just experiencing individuality temporarily.

Across Traditions

Consciousness Beyond Body

"Whether it was in the body or out of the body I do not know — God knows."— Paul, 2 Corinthians 12:2 (Christianity)

Paul describing his own OBE — ascension to "third heaven." Classic out-of-body confusion: he wasn't sure if he was in or out of body. Christian mystics (St. Teresa of Avila, St. John of the Cross) documented ecstatic visions, soul flights, union with the divine.

Hinduism — Subtle Body Travel: the sukshma sharira (subtle body) can leave the sthula sharira (physical body). Yogis practise this through meditation, pranayama, kundalini. Patanjali's Yoga Sutras describe siddhis including travel to distant locations and communication with higher beings.

Tibetan Buddhism — Dream Yoga: maintaining consciousness during sleep. The Tibetan Book of the Dead describes bardo states — between-death realms that map directly onto Monroe's Focus 22–27.

Shamanism — Soul Flight: core practice across Siberian, Native American, African, and South American traditions. The shaman's consciousness leaves body to journey to upper and lower worlds, retrieve lost souls, communicate with spirits.

Ancient Egypt — Ba and Ka: Ba (consciousness/soul) leaves Ka (physical body) at night to travel to Duat (the astral realm). The Book of the Dead provides instructions for navigating afterlife dimensions.

Daoism — Shen Travel: spirit leaving body during deep meditation, travelling to celestial realms, meeting immortals.

The Pattern

Christians called it "caught up to third heaven." Hindus: "subtle body travel." Buddhists: "dream yoga." Shamans: "soul flight." Egyptians: "Ba leaving Ka." Daoists: "Shen travel." Monroe: "out-of-body experience." Same phenomenon. Every culture. Every era. Different names.
